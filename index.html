Emilio Ortiz Avila

🌱 Bio

Hi! I’m Emilio, a microbiologist focused on bioinformatics, transcriptomics, and computational biology.

My academic work has centered on the analysis of RNA-seq data in non-model organisms. During my thesis work at the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), I studied differential gene expression associated with the regenerative response of the terrestrial gastropod Deroceras laeve. This project allowed me to strengthen my experience in transcriptomic workflows, omics data analysis, and reproducible computational approaches for biological research.

Beyond research, I have been involved in teaching, bioinformatics training, and scientific outreach. I have contributed to the development of learning materials and the organization of academic activities for students beginning in computational biology.

I am also part of ISCB RSG-Mexico, where I participate in community-driven initiatives related to bioinformatics education, workshops, symposia, journal clubs, and outreach activities.

Currently, I continue expanding my skills in bioinformatics, computational biology, Python, and data analysis through research, teaching, training programs, and collaborative academic communities.
